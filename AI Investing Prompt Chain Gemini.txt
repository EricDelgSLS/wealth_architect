AI Portfolio Builder GV2
The whole point of this is to help you build a real, long-term investment plan that you can feel good about. The prompt chain is supposed to educate you about why and what you are potentially investing in. 
The strategy here is about using smart, proven ideas. No hype, no guessing, and definitely no trying to "time the market." Think of this portfolio as your core wealth-building engine, the kind of thing that grows in the background without you having to stress over it or check it every day.
This doesn't mean you can't do the casino type investing trying to score big wins. If you want to have a separate pot of "fuck it money" to chase stocks or crypto just track that separately and make sure its not too much of your general portfolio. Just think of this portfolio as something different.
The goal is simple: build a balanced portfolio that, over the long haul, should get you better growth than just letting your money sit in a high-yield savings account or even a basic S&P 500 (VOO) fund.


* Obligatory: This is not investment advice, invest at your own risk and don’t get mad at me if the market crashes please. Remember, this is a long term play, you’ll be alright. Or not, who knows. 






























NOTES (READ-ME)


1. Initial Setup Questions
When answering the first set of questions be as thorough as possible, if you have sector knowledge or any general info about a sector you want to influence your portfolio make sure to describe it in detail. 


Example (my AI tilt):
 I use AI a lot at work and believe in its long-term impact, so I asked the model to lean a bit heavier into AI’s “picks-and-shovels” — the infrastructure and tooling behind AI (semiconductors, compute, cloud, networking, and software platforms that others build on). I still keep core broad-market exposure, but I add a tilt toward the companies powering the AI stack.
If you’re in semiconductors, engineering, defense, healthcare, etc., tell the model that. Ask it to reflect your edge by slightly overweighting those areas (with guardrails) and to explain the tradeoffs. Or don’t. 
2. Post Portfolio Adjustments
After the AI builds your first portfolio, keep the conversation going. If it feels too conservative, ask it to shift weight from broad ETFs (e.g., VOO/VTI) into single stocks or new sectors you want (defense, banking, clean energy, etc.). Ask which ETFs represent those sectors and why.


3. Keep Momentum With A GPT Project Folder (Shared Memory)
Create a project folder in GPT (e.g., “My Long-Term Investment Portfolio”) and save this chat there. GPT’s shared memory keeps your initial goals, risk profile, and sector notes, so future sessions start with context. Use the same folder to:
* Rebalance or tweak tilts (e.g., “shift 5% from VOO into semis ETF”).
* Add new ideas (“What are some other sectors I should look into adding?”).
* Log changes (“version this as v1.1 and summarize what changed”).
* Ask “why” questions (“explain how this affects risk and drawdowns”).
This makes the portfolio a living document: you can leave it alone for months or come back anytime and build from the same foundation.

* **Guardrails over Predictions:** You never "time" the market. Instead, you provide context on the current economic environment while building a resilient portfolio designed to succeed over years and decades, not weeks.
* **Simplicity & Clarity:** Avoid jargon where possible. If you must use a technical term, explain it simply. The final plan should be easily understood by a novice.
### SAFETY FIRST (NON-NEGOTIABLE GUARDRAILS) ###
1. **Emergency Fund Check:** You must ask if the user has an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of living expenses. If not, you will strongly advise them to build this fund (perhaps in a High-Yield Savings Account) before investing significant amounts.

3. **Concentration Risk Limits:** In the final portfolio, no single stock may exceed 5% of the total, and no single thematic sector (e.g., Clean Energy, Defense) may exceed 15%. The core of the portfolio must be diversified.
